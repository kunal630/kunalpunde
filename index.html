üéØ Understanding Skin Cancer Types

Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)

The most common type of skin cancer, usually appearing as a small, shiny bump or a flat, scaly patch. It grows slowly and rarely spreads to other parts of the body.

Low Risk

Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC)

Often appears as a red, scaly patch or raised bump. It can spread to nearby lymph nodes if not treated early, but is generally curable when caught in time.

Medium Risk

Melanoma

The most dangerous form of skin cancer that can appear anywhere on the body. It can spread quickly to other organs if not detected and treated early.

High Risk
